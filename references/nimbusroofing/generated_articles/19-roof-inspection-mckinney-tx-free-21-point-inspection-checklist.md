## Nimbus AI-Roofing Agent’s Free 21-Point Inspection Checklist

Our proprietary 21-Point Inspection Checklist is a systematic, top-to-bottom evaluation that goes far beyond a simple visual check. It is designed to identify both obvious and hidden damage, ensuring the complete integrity of your roofing system.

### H3: The Roof Surface: Shingles and Materials

1.  **Shingle Granule Loss:** Checking for excessive granule loss, which reduces the shingle's ability to reflect UV rays and protect the asphalt.
2.  **Cracked or Broken Shingles:** Identifying shingles damaged by wind, hail, or thermal shock, which are direct entry points for water.
3.  **Missing Shingles:** Documenting areas where shingles have been blown off, exposing the underlayment.
4.  **Blistering and Curling:** Assessing signs of material aging and moisture entrapment, common in the McKinney heat.
5.  **Ponding Water Areas:** Identifying flat roof sections where water accumulates, leading to premature membrane failure.

### H3: Flashing and Penetrations: The Vulnerable Points

6.  **Chimney Flashing:** Inspecting the metal flashing around the chimney for separation, rust, or improper sealing.
7.  **Vent Pipe Boots:** Checking the rubber or plastic seals around plumbing and exhaust vents for cracking and deterioration.
8.  **Skylight Seals:** Examining the perimeter of skylights for compromised seals and potential leaks.
9.  **Wall and Valley Flashing:** Ensuring all metal valleys and wall-to-roof transitions are properly installed and free of debris or corrosion.
10. **Siding and Trim Interface:** Checking where the roof meets vertical surfaces for proper counter-flashing.

### H3: Drainage and Water Management

11. **Gutter and Downspout Functionality:** Inspecting for blockages, proper slope, and secure attachment to ensure efficient water runoff.
12. **Fascia and Soffit Condition:** Checking for signs of water damage, rot, or pest intrusion in the roof's edge components.
13. **Drip Edge Installation:** Ensuring the drip edge is correctly installed to direct water away from the fascia and into the gutters.

### H3: Structural and Interior Integrity

14. **Attic Ventilation:** Assessing the balance of intake and exhaust ventilation to prevent heat and moisture buildup, which can damage the decking and shorten shingle life.
15. **Decking and Sheathing:** Checking the attic space for signs of water stains, sagging, or mold growth on the roof deck.
16. **Rafter and Truss Condition:** Inspecting the structural components for any signs of movement, damage, or water intrusion.
17. **Insulation Assessment:** Evaluating the attic insulation for dampness or compression, which indicates a leak or ventilation issue.

### H3: Safety and Workmanship

18. **Hail and Wind Damage Documentation:** Systematically marking and photographing all evidence of storm damage for insurance purposes.
19. **Previous Repair Quality:** Assessing the quality and effectiveness of any prior repairs to ensure they meet current standards.
20. **Overall Roof Slope and Pitch:** Confirming the roof's design is effectively shedding water.
21. **Comprehensive Report Generation:** Providing a detailed, photographic report of all findings, complete with repair recommendations and an estimate.

## Common Roofing Issues Identified by a Professional Roof Inspection McKinney TX

The specific climate and geography of McKinney, TX, lead to a few common culprits when it comes to roof damage. Our 21-Point Inspection is specifically tailored to detect these local issues.

### H3: Hail Damage: The Silent Killer

Hail damage is often invisible from the ground. Even small hail can bruise shingles, dislodging protective granules and creating weak spots that lead to premature failure. Our inspectors are trained to spot the tell-tale signs of hail impact, which are critical for a successful insurance claim.

### H3: Wind Uplift and Shingle Blow-Off

High winds, common during North Texas thunderstorms, can lift and loosen shingles, especially along the edges and ridges. If left unaddressed, this can lead to large sections of the roof being compromised. Our inspection checks the integrity of the shingle seal and the security of all perimeter components.

### H3: Thermal Shock and UV Degradation

The extreme heat and intense UV rays in McKinney cause materials to expand and contract rapidly (thermal shock), leading to cracking, warping, and accelerated aging. This is why we pay close attention to the condition of vent pipe boots and flashing, which are often the first to fail under these conditions.

### H3: Poor Ventilation and Moisture Issues

A poorly ventilated attic can trap heat and moisture, effectively cooking your roof from the inside out. This dramatically shortens the lifespan of your shingles and can lead to mold and mildew in the attic. Our inspection includes a thorough check of your ventilation system (Item 14) to ensure proper airflow.
