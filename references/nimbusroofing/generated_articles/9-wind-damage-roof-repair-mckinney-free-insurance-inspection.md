## The Hidden Dangers of Wind Damage to Your McKinney Roof

Wind damage is often a subtle, insidious force that compromises your roof layer by layer. High winds can lift, crease, and tear shingles, creating vulnerabilities that may not be visible from the ground. For McKinney property owners, understanding the mechanics of wind damage is the first step toward proactive protection and effective repair.

### Understanding the Types of Wind Damage (Lift, Crease, Tear-Off)

Wind creates both positive and negative pressure on a roof. This combination loosens sealant bonds, causing shingles to lift and flap. This repeated motion leads to creasing, weakening the shingle, or, in severe cases, a complete tear-off, exposing the underlayment. Even if the shingle settles back down, the damage significantly reduces the roof's ability to shed water.

### Why Immediate Inspection is Crucial (Preventing Secondary Water Damage)

The most critical reason for an immediate, professional inspection is to prevent secondary water damage. A lifted or creased shingle creates a direct pathway for water intrusion, which can penetrate the decking and interior of your home, leading to expensive repairs. A swift response is paramount.
