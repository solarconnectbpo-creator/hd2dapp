### FAQ Section: Your McKinney Storm Damage Roofing Questions Answered

Here are some of the most common questions we receive from homeowners dealing with **storm damage roofing McKinney** and the insurance claim process.

#### Q: How long do I have to file a roof insurance claim in Texas?
A: While most Texas policies allow up to a year from the date of loss, it is always best to file immediately. Delays can complicate the process and lead to further damage that may not be covered.

#### Q: Will filing a claim for storm damage roofing McKinney raise my insurance rates?
A: Generally, filing a claim for a catastrophic, weather-related event (like hail or wind) that affects a large area will not single-handedly cause your individual rates to increase. Rate increases are typically based on widespread claims in your area.

#### Q: What is the difference between ACV and RCV in my claim?
A: ACV (Actual Cash Value) is the cost of the damaged item minus depreciation. RCV (Replacement Cost Value) is the cost to replace the item with a new one. Most policies are RCV, but the insurance company often pays the ACV first, and the remaining depreciation is paid after the work is completed and invoiced.
